    Althaf Shajihan, Ph.D.

    Assistant Professor

    Civil, Construction, and Environmental Engineering

    Ph.D., Civil Engineering, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), 2025
    M.S., Computer Science, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), 2024
    M.S., Structural Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology Bombay (IITB), 2018
    B.S., Civil Engineering, National Institute of Technology Calicut (NITC), 2016

    Dr. Shajihan is an Assistant Professor of Civil, Construction, and Environmental Engineering at San Diego State University (SDSU) where he directs the PRISM Lab (Physics-informed Resilient Infrastructure and Structural Modeling Lab). With over 10+ years of expertise in Structural Engineering, Dr. Shajihan specializes in developing artificial intelligence, computer vision, smart sensing, and deep learning techniques for structural condition assessment applications. Dr. Shajihan also holds an Affiliate Faculty appointment in the Computational Science Research Center at SDSU. He has collaborated on numerous projects with federal agencies such as NSF, DoD, DoT, and FRA, alongside industry partners. Dr. Shajihan has received several accolades, including the prestigious Kinra Fellowship at UIUC, and has published extensively in top-tier journals.
